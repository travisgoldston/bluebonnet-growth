Why this feels so hard

You can be great… and still invisible.

Most local businesses don’t lose calls because they’re bad at their trade. They lose calls because Google can’t tell who to trust.

Up Highway 75, the competition has changed. McKinney got bigger. Melissa and Anna keep growing. Sherman is steady and established. Meanwhile Frisco, Prosper, and Celina are booming—and that usually means more ads, more spam, and more “SEO guys.”

If you’ve been burned before, you’re not crazy. A lot of agencies sell you a dashboard and call it “results.” We do the opposite: plain language, clear work, and a direct line to us.

Reality check

If you’re not on the map, you don’t exist.

That sounds harsh. It’s also how people behave. They search “roofer near me” or “dentist in Allen” and pick from what shows up first.

Your job isn’t to “game Google.” Your job is to make it easy for Google (and real people) to understand: what you do, where you do it, and why you’re the safe call.

The simple version

  • Google needs clear signals (not guesses)
  • People need a fast, easy-to-call website
  • Both need proof that you’re the real deal

Services

Three things that work better together

Most “SEO” falls apart because the website is confusing. Most “web design” falls apart because nobody can find it. We do both, plus the conversion fixes that turn traffic into calls.

SEO that’s built for calls

Local SEO is mostly three things: your map listing, your pages, and your proof (reviews, consistency, and trust signals).

  • Map visibility for your service areas (McKinney to Sherman and beyond)
  • Service pages that match what people actually search
  • Fixes that make Google confident you’re real

If you want a “free visibility check,” this is what we’ll look at first. Request one here.

Good fit for

Plumbers, HVAC, roofers, cleaners, therapists, dentists, contractors, landscapers.

If you serve a specific city (like Allen or Wylie) and you’re not showing up in the map pack, SEO is usually step one.

Web design that doesn’t get in the way

Your website shouldn’t be “pretty.” It should be clear. It should load fast. And on a phone, it should make calling feel obvious.

  • Fast, mobile-first pages
  • Clear “who we help / what we do / where we serve” messaging
  • Simple layouts that don’t bury the phone number

Need a rebuild? Web design is usually a one-time add-on. We’ll tell you if you can keep what you have.

Quick reality

If your site is slow, confusing, or hard to use on a phone, SEO gets more expensive. Fixing the site makes everything else work better.

Conversion optimization (the “don’t waste clicks” work)

Sometimes you already have traffic. It just doesn’t turn into calls. That’s usually messaging, layout, or trust—not “more content.”

  • Fix the top pages people land on
  • Make contact options obvious (call, text, form)
  • Clean up friction: confusing menus, buried CTAs, weak proof

If you’re in Plano or Frisco and clicks are expensive, conversion fixes can pay back fast.

Not always a fit

If you’re getting almost no traffic, conversion work comes later. First we get you seen.
