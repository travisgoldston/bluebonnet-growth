Areas I serve

Local SEO and web design across North Texas

I work with small businesses across North Texas—mostly owners who want more calls from nearby customers without turning their life into full-time marketing.

Cities I focus on

These are the main cities I’ve built dedicated pages for. If you serve several of them, that’s normal. Most of my clients do.

McKinney

Fast-growing city along 75 and 380 with strong competition in home services, medical, and trades. A key market for local visibility.

Melissa

Home base. A small but fast-growing 75 corridor town where word of mouth is giving way to Google searches.

Frisco

Fast-growing suburb with heavy competition in every category. Great fit if you want to stand out in a crowded search result.

Allen

Busy corridors, strong household income, and customers who expect polished websites and clear Google listings.

Plano

Established market with crowded search results. Great fit if you need to compete against strong brands and long-standing competitors.

Wylie

Growing east-side city where word of mouth is strong, but more people are starting their search online.

Murphy

A small, family-focused pocket where customers compare options across nearby cities and choose whoever looks easiest to trust online.

Sachse

Close to everything, which means you’re compared to everything. Clear listings and a solid website make a big difference here.

Lucas

Small town feel with suburban search behavior. The goal is to show up clearly when people look for what you do nearby.

Anna

One of the fastest-growing spots north of McKinney. New residents start with Google, which makes early visibility a big advantage.

Van Alstyne

Small-town feel along 75 where early online visibility is a big advantage as more families move in.

Gunter

North of Prosper and Celina, with plenty of room to grow and a need for local names to show up clearly.

Prosper

Affluent suburb between Frisco and Celina where customers expect polished websites and strong Google presence.

Celina

Fast-growing market where new demand meets growing competition. Being easy to find (and easy to choose) is the difference.

Sherman

Established regional hub with a mix of legacy businesses and newer competition in almost every service.

Denison

Strong local market that overlaps heavily with Sherman. Google visibility helps you compete when customers search across the corridor.

Howe

Small-town market where Google often blends results with nearby cities. Getting the basics right helps local businesses win.
