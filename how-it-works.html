Step 1

Soil check (audit)

Before we change anything, we look at what you already have—website, listings, reviews, and the gap between how you talk about the work and how customers search for it.

This isn’t a lecture. It’s a clear picture of where you’re losing people: slow pages, missing service pages, wrong categories, or a profile that doesn’t match how you actually serve your town.

  • Site health and mobile experience
  • Google Business Profile and local signals
  • Where calls and forms are leaking

If this sounds like your situation, the home page spells out why “invisible” hurts before you ever spend a dollar.

What you get

A prioritized list: what to fix first, what can wait, and what would be a waste of your time. You’ll know why—not just “trust us.”

Step 2

Planting (setup)

We put the basics in the ground: accurate business info, a site structure that matches how people hire you, and messaging that sounds like you—not a generic template.

If the soil is wrong, nothing else takes root. Planting is about making sure the first impression matches the real business.

  • Listings and citations cleaned up
  • Core service pages and contact paths
  • Clear “who we are / what we do” story

Local SEO, content, and your website all show up here—we sequence the work so you’re not paying to polish a broken front door.

Curious how hands-on it is? Read about Bluebonnet—you work with the person doing the work, not a rotating cast.

What “planted” means

You can point to something solid: correct hours, a site that loads, and pages that match how people actually hire you—not a pile of half-finished tasks.

Step 3

Tending (optimization)

Once the foundation is honest, we tighten what gets in the way: headlines that don’t match search intent, pages that don’t answer real questions, forms that fail on a phone, or photos that don’t look like your crew.

Tending is ongoing judgment—what to improve this month, what to leave alone, and what to measure so we’re not guessing.

  • On-page clarity and internal links
  • Content that answers how people actually ask
  • Conversion fixes (calls, texts, forms)

The home page version of this step lives in the growth path grid—jump back anytime.

Step 4

Blooming (visibility)

This is when neighbors start finding you in the moments that matter: search, maps, and the quick comparisons people make on their phones in a driveway.

Blooming isn’t magic. It’s the result of accurate information, helpful pages, and proof that you’re the real deal. We don’t chase tricks—we stack things that still count next quarter.

Step 5

Harvest (results)

Harvest means measuring what you care about: calls, booked jobs, repeat customers, and the quality of conversations—not charts that don’t connect to payroll.

  • What to watch week to week
  • What to double down on
  • When to say “this is working—leave it alone”

If something isn’t moving, you’ll hear it from me. We adjust, or we part ways cleanly—no long-term handcuffs.

How services map in

Where Local SEO, content, and your site fit

You don’t buy “packages” off a shelf. You move through the path, and the right mix shows up at the right time.

  • Local SEO — strongest in soil check, planting, and blooming: listings, relevance, and local proof.
  • Content strategy — heaviest in planting and tending: the story, FAQs, and pages that earn trust.
  • Growth-focused websites — often starts in soil check and planting, then keeps paying off in tending and harvest.
